What to Include on the Referral

A complete referral helps us triage appropriately and avoids delays. Where possible, please include:

Reason for referral and clinical question
Recent PSA (if applicable)
Relevant imaging reports (ultrasound, CT, MRI)
Current medications, especially anticoagulants
Whether the patient is catheter-dependent or in retention
Which pathway is most appropriate (prostate, hematuria, renal mass, stones, retention)
Referring physician email (for faster correspondence)

Cases We Prioritize

The following referrals are triaged on a priority basis. Please indicate urgency clearly on the referral.

Gross Hematuria / Bladder Mass

Visible blood in the urine requires investigation. Patients with frank hematuria or a bladder mass seen on imaging should be referred for cystoscopy and workup without delay.

Elevated PSA with Suspicious Findings

PSA above 3.0 ng/mL or abnormal DRE findings. Rapid access to transperineal biopsy when tissue sampling is indicated.

Renal Mass on Imaging

A suspicious renal mass identified on ultrasound, CT, or MRI should be assessed by a urologic surgeon in a timely manner. If your patient has a new renal mass finding, we are happy to see them promptly.

Urinary Retention

Patients who are catheter-dependent or unable to void are prioritized for both consultation and trial of void. Please flag on the referral.

Obstructing Ureteral Stone

Confirmed stone on CT/ultrasound with pain or hydronephrosis. Note: Fever with obstruction is a medical emergency — send to the Emergency Department first for IV antibiotics and urgent drainage.

For Patients

A referral from your family physician or nurse practitioner is required. If you would like to be seen at our clinic, please ask your doctor to send a referral to the fax number or email address above. If your situation is urgent, ask them to mark it as such.
